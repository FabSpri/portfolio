An I5 6600K and its "package"

The seller of this CPU was aware the board was not functional and offered it as a "package" to transport the CPU. I happily accepted the deal. I noticed a blown NMOSFET right away (Fig. 1), which made me kinda question my purchase. The seller convinced me however, that the CPU was never actually ran on this specific board. Indeed it turned out to be fully funcional. The damage on the motherboard confused me somewhat however. One usually wouldn´t expect them to blow on a board like this, considering its basically an entry level budget product, known to house CPUs with according power needs. Also these MOSFETS are still tough nuts to crack with plenty of headroom to boot. Overclocking and voltage regulation are not an option on H110 boards.

There wasn´t a short on the core voltage rail. Instead the 12V rail fused itself to the gate. Initial measurements of the periphery and the controller proved unremarkable however. I fully expected, that i would have to pry the FET from the PCB. It came off without a problem though with intact pads underneath.

I currently don´t have any MOSFETS with according specs at hand to replace the damaged part, so this thing will probably end up as a parts donor.

UPDATE: I now got proper replacement for the missing MOSFET. I didn´t really expect the board to boot up once i replaced it...but it did! Still have some digging to do here, but maybe it could become the basis for a low-profile build.
