Technological innovation and rapid release cycles have been a defining element of human societies and markets for decades. Albeit the impressive advancements, the question might present itlsef, wether we haven´t entered an age of diminishing returns when it comes to consumer tech, be it due to shorter lifetime and high demand for cheaper low-quality products or questionable upgrade cycles in regards to quantifiable consumer needs and performance gains.

Vast amounts of energy and resources are consumed in establishing each new generation of technological consumer products, while e-waste is the fastest growing subcategory within global waste disposal systems. And though there have been promissing developments in terms of recycling procedures, they remain rather inefficient in terms of resoruce recuperation, energy consumption and economic viability, while posing taxing challenges regarding environmental and societal impact, especially in emerging markets.

So why not maintain these products, if there are use cases and demand? And of both there is plenty in the age of digitaliation and growing social and economic inequality. Though energy efficiency remains a part of the equation, there are high-turnover second hand markets for consumer grade tech products, that ensure participation in technological societies. And yet many of these products get discarded and end up in landfills or inefficient recycling processes once an often minor technical defect occurs that impacts basic functionality. This defect might be easy and cheap to fix, the diagnostic process however often exceeds the limits of economic viability.




So that´s where i come in while going through periods of greater family responsibilities in recent years. I buy disfunctional consumer grade products and fix them on a non- to low-profit basis while reintroducing them to local second hand markets and platforms. I mostly dabble in consumer electronics and IT hardware and perform repairs based on the basics of system integration and electronics.

Another pet-peeve of mine is tweaking PC hardware to higher standards of energy efficiency, be it through BIOS, driver or hardware modifications. Manufacturers often apply generous margins in the design of power delivery systems to ensure functionality under variables occuring during production, parts-sourcing or technical specifications and properties of the corresponding periphery. In many instances one can reduce power consumption significantly while maintaining stable performance metrics.
